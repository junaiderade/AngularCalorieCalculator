Step 1: Calculate BMR

Basal metabolic rate is calculated using the Mifflin-St Jeor Equation, which is seen as the most scientifically accurate method to calculate BMR today. For males, this formula is 10(weight) + 6.25(height) + 5. For females, it is 10(weight) + 6.25(height) - 161.
Step 2: Add Activity Level Multiplier

Based on the activity level you enter, your base metabolic rate is multiplied to get your maintenance calories (not accounting for exercise yet).

  • Sedentary: BMR * 1.2
  • Lightly Active: BMR * 1.375
  • Active: BMR * 1.55
  • Very Active: BMR * 1.725
Step 3: Account for Calories Burned during Exercise

Here's the tricky part. The calculator multiplies how many days a week you exercise by how many calories you burn on average per day and then divides that number by 7 (because there are 7 days a week) to get the number of extra calories you need to maintain your weight based on your exercise. This number is added to step 2 to get your final maintenance calorie calculation. For example if I go to the gym 5 days a week and burn 500 calories each day, I need 2500 extra calories this week to maintain myself. And 2500/7 = 357, so I need surplus of 357 calories a day to keep me at the same weight.

Step 4: Calculate Goal Calories

Finally, it takes how many pounds you want to lose/gain this week, multiples that by 3500 (because there are 3500 calories in a pound of body mass), divides that number by 7, and either adds or subtracts it to your maintenance calories to get you your goal calories. For example, if I want to lose .5 pounds this week, that means eat 1750 less calories in total this week, which means I should aim to eat 250 calories less than my maintenance calories each day.
